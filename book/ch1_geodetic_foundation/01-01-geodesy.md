# 1.1 Geodesy and the Shape of the Earth

Modern geodesy represents the profound intersection of geometry, geophysics, and relativistic physics. The discipline is traditionally defined by its three fundamental pillars: the measurement of the Earth's geometric shape, the orientation of the Earth in space, and the determination of the Earth's gravity field. In the era of satellite navigation, quantum sensing, and high-performance digital elevation modeling, a fourth pillar has emerged: the precise determination of time.
